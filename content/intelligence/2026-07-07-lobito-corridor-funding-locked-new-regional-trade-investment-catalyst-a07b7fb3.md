The Africa Finance Corporation has successfully closed a $753 million financing package, fully funding the rehabilitation and operation of the 1,300-kilometer Lobito Corridor railway. This US-backed project will link Angola to the Democratic Republic of Congo border, establishing a crucial new transport route.

This financing unlocks a critical artery for mineral exports from DRC and Zambia, significantly reducing logistics costs and transit times. It enhances the viability of existing and new mining projects, opening opportunities in supporting infrastructure, logistics, and adjacent economic zones. The US backing adds geopolitical weight, signalling long-term commitment and stability.

Investors should monitor progress on construction and operational timelines. Key indicators will include increased foreign direct investment into DRC and Zambian mining, alongside developments in port infrastructure at Lobito and new industrial cluster formation along the corridor. This project's success could catalyse similar regional infrastructure initiatives.
