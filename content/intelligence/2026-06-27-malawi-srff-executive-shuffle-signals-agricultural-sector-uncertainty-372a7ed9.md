Malawi's government abruptly removed Paul Taulo as the newly appointed CEO of the Smallholder Revolving Fertiliser Fund (SRFF) amidst credibility controversies. Taulo, in the role for barely a month, has been replaced by Itaye.

This swift leadership change signals persistent governance instability and potential operational disruption within a critical agricultural parastatal. For investors, such rapid executive turnovers create uncertainty around policy direction, particularly concerning fertilizer subsidies and agricultural input supply chains. It highlights a recurring risk in managing key state-run entities.
