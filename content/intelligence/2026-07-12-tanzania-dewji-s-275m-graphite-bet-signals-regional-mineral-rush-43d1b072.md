Mohammed Dewji's MeTL Group is committing $275 million to a new graphite mining operation in Tanzania. This significant investment from one of East Africa's largest homegrown conglomerates is set to see commercial production begin within 18 months, directly engaging in the intensifying global competition for battery materials.

This substantial local capital injection signals strong confidence in Tanzania's critical mineral potential and the robust long-term demand for battery inputs. It positions Tanzania as an emerging key player in the global energy transition supply chain. While immediate focus is on raw material export, MeTL's broader interest in local battery processing highlights a significant industrialization opportunity for the region.

Investors should closely monitor MeTL's project development timelines and any further announcements regarding their local processing capabilities. We also recommend watching for potential policy shifts or incentives from the Tanzanian government aimed at supporting domestic value addition in critical minerals. This move could well catalyze additional major domestic or international investments in the East African mining sector.
