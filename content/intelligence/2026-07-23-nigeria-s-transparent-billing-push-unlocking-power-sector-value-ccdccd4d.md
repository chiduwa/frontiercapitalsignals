Nigerian electricity consumers, both households and businesses, have long faced arbitrary estimated billing, leading to widespread complaints and dissatisfaction. A new initiative, potentially spearheaded by "Tegbe", aims to democratise and introduce transparency to the electricity billing system, signaling a potential shift from the current opaque practices.

This move is critical for investor confidence and sector viability. Arbitrary billing creates revenue uncertainty for utilities and discourages investment in critical power infrastructure. Transparent, metered billing can boost revenue assurance, improve liquidity for power companies, and provide cost certainty for businesses, making the sector more attractive.

Investors should closely track the rollout and enforcement of this transparent billing pathway. Key indicators include the pace of metering deployment, utility compliance, and the tangible impact on collection rates and overall grid stability. Look for specific policy frameworks or regulatory changes that underpin this democratisation effort.
