Kenyan High-Net-Worth Individuals (HNWIs) are significantly reducing direct property exposure, instead prioritizing farmland, data centers, and liquid financial assets. This strategic shift, highlighted by Knight Frank research, reflects a move towards more dynamic and inflation-resilient investments.

This signals a maturing local investor landscape, with HNWIs seeking both inflation hedges through agriculture and high-growth opportunities in digital infrastructure. For international investors, it validates farmland as a robust asset class in Kenya and underscores the increasing demand for secure, high-yield digital infrastructure plays, mirroring global trends.

Monitor capital flows into agricultural land and the pipeline of new data center projects. Pay close attention to policy changes impacting land tenure, agricultural subsidies, and digital infrastructure incentives, as these will shape future investment landscapes and competitive advantages. Local partnerships in these sectors will also become increasingly valuable.
