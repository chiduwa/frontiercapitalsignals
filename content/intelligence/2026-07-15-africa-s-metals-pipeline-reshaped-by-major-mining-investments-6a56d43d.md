Five mining firms are significantly advancing copper, cobalt, and gold projects across Zambia, DRC, Ghana, and South Africa. KoBold Metals notably commenced work on its $2 billion Mingomba project, while Makor Resources deployed $2-$3 million in Zambia. Buenassa is also progressing copper-cobalt refining capabilities, reflecting a push for new deposits and domestic processing.

This activity underscores increasing investor confidence in Africa's critical mineral sector and its long-term supply chain relevance. The focus on domestic processing and formalised production systems creates downstream opportunities and reduces export risk. Demand for these metals, crucial for the global energy transition, will only grow.

Investors should closely track project development timelines, particularly those in our target markets like Ghana. Monitor government policies supporting value addition, infrastructure investments, and potential for local equity participation. Watch for further announcements on off-take agreements and financing structures.
