Dangote Refinery has swiftly reverted to dollar pricing for domestic petroleum products, leaving Nigerian petrol retailers struggling. This move follows an earlier attempt to accept Naira and highlights the immediate impact of Nigeria's newly deregulated energy sector on currency mandates.

This development is a critical signal for investors. It underscores the immense difficulty of sustaining local currency mandates even for key domestic industries, reflecting deep-seated challenges with Naira stability and liquidity. Businesses reliant on these essential products face increased operational costs and significant foreign exchange risk.

Monitor the Central Bank of Nigeria's response to this implicit dollarization by a major domestic player. Watch for any policy shifts regarding FX allocation and enforcement of local currency transactions in critical sectors. The viability of local businesses and broader inflation trends will be key indicators of the market's adaptation to these currency pressures.
