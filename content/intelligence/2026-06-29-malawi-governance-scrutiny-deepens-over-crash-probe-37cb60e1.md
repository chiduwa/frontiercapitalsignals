Former National Intelligence Service Director General Dokani Ngwira abruptly halted his testimony before Malawi's Parliamentary Ad Hoc Committee investigating a June 10 military plane crash. Ngwira cited a 'clearance row' as the reason for refusing to provide full evidence, delaying a critical probe.

This development raises concerns for international investors regarding institutional transparency, governance, and the rule of law in Malawi. Lack of cooperation in high-profile investigations can signal underlying political instability or weak accountability mechanisms, elevating perceived political risk and potentially deterring foreign direct investment.

Investors should closely monitor the resolution of this clearance issue and the broader progress of the parliamentary inquiry. Observe government responses to ensure institutional integrity and watch for any wider implications on political stability or public confidence, which could impact long-term market sentiment.
