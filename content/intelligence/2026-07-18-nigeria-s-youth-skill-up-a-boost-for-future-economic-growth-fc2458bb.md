Tomorrow Foundation, Thunderbird School of Global Management, and Arizona State University have partnered to launch the 100 Million Learners Initiative in Nigeria. This three-year program aims to equip Nigerian youth with critical entrepreneurial and leadership skills for the future economy.

This initiative directly addresses a significant barrier to sustained growth: human capital development. A more skilled Nigerian workforce enhances productivity, attracts foreign direct investment, and fuels consumer spending and innovation. Investors should see this as a long-term positive for Nigeria's economic landscape and market potential.

Monitor the initiative's rollout and its success metrics. Look for emerging sectors benefiting from this skilled labor pool, particularly in technology, entrepreneurship, and services. Evaluate broader government support and private sector engagement to gauge sustained growth in human capital development.
