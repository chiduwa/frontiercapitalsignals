Nigeria is now Sub-Saharan Africa's largest small-scale solar market. A new BloombergNEF report confirms businesses and households are driving this boom, adopting distributed solar solutions to circumvent an unreliable national grid. Utility-scale solar investment, however, remains stalled.

This signifies a robust, organic market for decentralized energy. Investors should focus on opportunities within solar panel distribution, installation, and financing for commercial, industrial, and residential sectors. The underlying driver, persistent grid unreliability, ensures continued demand for alternative power solutions across Nigeria.

Watch for potential policy shifts or regulatory frameworks that could further enable or constrain this distributed energy growth. Observe how established energy players respond to this market bypass. Also, monitor for consolidation within the burgeoning small-scale solar service and supply ecosystem.
