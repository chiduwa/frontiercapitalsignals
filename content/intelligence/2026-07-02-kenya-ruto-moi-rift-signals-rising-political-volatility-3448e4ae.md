President Ruto has publicly assailed The Standard newspaper, confirming the breakdown of his political alliance with Gideon Moi. This escalation transforms a previous unexpected political truce into an open conflict, citing disputes over power, unfulfilled promises, and press freedom, all ahead of the 2027 elections.

This development signals increasing political instability and potential for volatility in Kenya. For investors, such open factionalism can undermine policy predictability and investor confidence. Concerns over press freedom, particularly involving a major media house, often indicate broader governance risks that could impact the operating environment.

Investors should closely track the trajectory of this political confrontation and its potential to influence government stability. Observe any further actions against media or political opposition, and watch for policy shifts that might emerge from heightened pre-election tensions as Kenya approaches 2027.
