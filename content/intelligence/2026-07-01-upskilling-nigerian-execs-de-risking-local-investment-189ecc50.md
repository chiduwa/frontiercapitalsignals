CEIBS Nigeria alumni are strongly advocating for executive education among Nigerian business leaders. Their push is driven by the need for local executives to gain greater exposure to global business models, particularly to effectively navigate China's expanding influence across trade, investment, and innovation within the region.

For investors, this signals a growing commitment to enhancing local business sophistication. Improved executive capabilities can translate into more strategic partnerships, better risk management, and a deeper understanding of market dynamics, making Nigerian businesses more attractive and reliable counterparts for international capital. It addresses a key soft infrastructure gap.

Monitor the uptake of such executive education programs and any policy support for professional development. Observe how this enhanced global perspective manifests in Nigerian companies' strategic decisions, particularly in their engagement with foreign partners and their approach to innovation and market expansion.
