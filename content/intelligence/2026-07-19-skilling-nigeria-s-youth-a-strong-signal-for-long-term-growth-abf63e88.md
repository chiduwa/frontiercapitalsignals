Tomorrow Foundation, in partnership with Thunderbird School of Global Management and Arizona State University, launched the 100 Million Learners Initiative in Nigeria. This three-year program aims to equip Nigerian youth with entrepreneurial and leadership skills crucial for the future economy.

This initiative directly addresses a critical human capital gap in Nigeria, a key factor for long-term economic growth and stability. A more skilled and entrepreneurial workforce enhances consumer purchasing power, fuels local innovation, and improves the attractiveness of Nigeria as an investment destination, particularly in tech and service sectors. It signals commitment to human capital development, reducing operational risks associated with skill shortages.

Monitor the program's initial reach and success metrics, specifically job creation or startup formation rates among graduates. Also, watch for government support or private sector participation that could scale the initiative or lead to similar programs. Such developments would indicate sustained improvement in Nigeria's human capital landscape.
