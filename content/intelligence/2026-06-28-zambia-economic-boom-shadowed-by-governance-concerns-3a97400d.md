Zambia's UPND government under President Hichilema is highlighting robust economic performance driven by a booming mining sector and record agricultural harvests. Despite these strong re-election arguments, a significant segment, particularly the youth, expresses disillusionment over stalled democratic reforms and perceived betrayals on promised freedoms.

For investors, this presents a mixed signal. Solid economic fundamentals and sector-specific growth opportunities remain attractive. However, the brewing social discontent among the youth signals potential political instability and uncertainty, which could affect long-term policy predictability and increase investment risk, especially leading into future electoral cycles.

Investors should closely monitor the government’s engagement with youth grievances and any concrete steps towards democratic reform. Track key legislative developments and the evolving political discourse. A failure to address these social pressures could escalate political risk, while proactive measures might solidify stability and investor confidence.
