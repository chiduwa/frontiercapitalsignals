Zambia's Minister of Finance, Situmbeko Musokotwane, has formally advised Ghana to implement restrictive laws on national debt accumulation. He referenced Zambia's own legislative success in empowering Parliament to limit the executive's discretion on future borrowing.

This recommendation signals a growing regional focus on fiscal discipline and debt sustainability. For Ghana, adopting similar frameworks would demonstrate a stronger commitment to prudent financial management, potentially improving investor confidence and lowering the perceived risk premium associated with its public debt.

Investors should closely observe Ghana's legislative response to this advice. Any concrete steps towards institutionalizing stricter debt ceilings or enhancing parliamentary oversight will be a key indicator for sovereign risk and long-term economic stability. Monitor parliamentary debates and policy pronouncements.
