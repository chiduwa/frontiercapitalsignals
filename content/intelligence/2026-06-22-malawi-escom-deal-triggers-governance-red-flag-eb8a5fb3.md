Calls are escalating for a full investigation into a significant ESCOM procurement deal in Malawi. The Centre for Public Accountability and Development (CPAD) reports credible evidence of political interference, pressuring authorities to probe how the process was handled.

This situation matters for investors as it highlights elevated governance risks within state-owned enterprises and public procurement. Unchecked political interference can undermine fair competition, increase project costs, and deter foreign direct investment by signaling a non-transparent operating environment.

Investors should closely monitor the government's response to these allegations and the depth of any subsequent investigation. Look for swift, decisive action and accountability measures, which will indicate Malawi's commitment to combating corruption and improving business predictability.
