The World Bank is under pressure from Washington to refocus its priorities. The institution is now expected to lean towards economic growth and energy security, potentially diluting its commitment to climate-specific goals. This puts its 45% climate finance target and any curbs on gas projects directly into question.

For investors in our target markets like Nigeria and Ghana, this signals a potential shift in project viability. Funding for traditional energy infrastructure, particularly gas, could see renewed World Bank support. Projects previously deemed less attractive due to climate concerns might now find easier pathways to financing, emphasizing immediate growth and energy access over strict decarbonization.

Track formal policy adjustments from the World Bank and subsequent changes in funding allocations across Africa. Monitor specific project approvals, especially for gas infrastructure and other growth-oriented initiatives. This pivot could reshape the development landscape for key sectors in Ghana, Nigeria, Kenya, Malawi, and Uganda.
