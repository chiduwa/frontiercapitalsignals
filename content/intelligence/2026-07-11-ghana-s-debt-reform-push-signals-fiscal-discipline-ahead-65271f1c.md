Zambia's Finance Minister, Situmbeko Musokotwane, advised Ghana to enact restrictive laws on debt accumulation. He highlighted Zambia's own experience, where Parliament was empowered to tie the government's hands, preventing past borrowing excesses.

This advice is significant for investors. It underscores a growing regional emphasis on fiscal responsibility. If Ghana adopts similar parliamentary oversight or debt ceiling legislation, it could signal a commitment to sustainable public finance, potentially enhancing credit ratings and attracting more stable foreign investment.

Investors should monitor any legislative initiatives or public statements from Ghana's Ministry of Finance or Parliament regarding new debt management frameworks. Concrete steps towards enforcing fiscal discipline will be key indicators of Ghana's commitment to long-term economic stability.
