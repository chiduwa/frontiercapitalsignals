Ghana has successfully secured $1.7 billion for the Accra-Kumasi Expressway, placing the funds into a dedicated Bank of Ghana account. Finance Minister Ato Forson confirmed the military has cleared 70 percent of the route, with procurement set to begin this September.

This fund allocation significantly de-risks the ambitious expressway project, underscoring Ghana's commitment to delivering critical infrastructure. It signals tangible opportunities for construction firms, logistics providers, and suppliers of building materials. Improved connectivity will also unlock new economic activity and real estate value along the corridor, benefiting wider investment portfolios.

Investors should monitor the imminent procurement phase for contract awards and partnership opportunities. Key indicators include the swift commencement of engineering work and actual construction, along with consistent progress on corridor clearance. Ghana's ability to maintain these updated timelines will be crucial for broader investor confidence in future large-scale projects.
