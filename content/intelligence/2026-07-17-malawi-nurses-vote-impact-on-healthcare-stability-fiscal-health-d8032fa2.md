Malawi's Nurses and Midwives Organisation (NONM) will hold leadership elections on November 27. Members will elect new leadership to address welfare concerns over the next four years.

This election impacts Malawi's healthcare sector stability and public fiscal health. A responsive NONM leadership could stabilize the crucial nursing workforce, mitigating strike risks and ensuring service continuity. Conversely, unresolved welfare issues might lead to industrial action, affecting healthcare delivery and increasing public spending pressures.

Investors should monitor the new NONM leadership's priorities and the government's engagement with their demands. Key indicators will include new wage negotiations, any proposed policy changes regarding nurse employment, and the overall stability of labor relations within the health sector.
