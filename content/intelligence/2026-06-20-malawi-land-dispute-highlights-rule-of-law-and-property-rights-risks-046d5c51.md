Developer GM Properties Limited is proceeding with evictions in Lilongwe's Area 26, despite a High Court order mandating compensation for residents first. This long-running land dispute, spanning over 18 months, has now seen residents directly appeal to President Arthur Peter Mutharika for intervention.

This incident underscores significant risks regarding the rule of law and property rights in Malawi. Non-compliance with judicial rulings by developers, and the necessity of presidential involvement, signals potential regulatory instability. Such scenarios can deter foreign direct investment by introducing uncertainty in land tenure and contract enforcement.

Investors should closely monitor President Mutharika's response. His action will indicate the government's commitment to upholding judicial decisions and protecting property rights. A resolution prioritizing due process and fair compensation would signal a more predictable and stable investment climate.
