Malawi's First Vice-President, Jane Ansah, has been effectively sidelined. She retains constitutional status and attends Cabinet, but all her practical portfolios, like Disaster Management, have been removed. This situation creates a constitutional limbo, stripping her office of real authority.

This signals underlying political instability and weak institutional clarity in Malawi. Such ambiguity in high office indicates potential for governance unpredictability and shifts in policy direction. Investors require clear, stable political structures to mitigate risk and ensure policy continuity.

Monitor further developments regarding the Vice-Presidency's role and any broader governmental restructuring. Observe how this power dynamic impacts policy implementation, particularly in sectors previously under the VP's purview. Pay attention to public statements or constitutional challenges that might clarify or exacerbate this institutional ambiguity.
