A warehouse wall under construction at GM Plastics Industries in Maone, Limbe Township, Malawi, collapsed, resulting in three fatalities and 21 injuries among workers. The accident involved 28 bricklayers and casual labourers on Thursday afternoon.

This incident highlights significant operational risks in Malawi's industrial construction sector, particularly concerning structural integrity and worker safety standards. Investors should factor in potential for project delays, increased compliance costs, and reputational exposure arising from such failures. It signals a need for rigorous due diligence on local construction practices.
