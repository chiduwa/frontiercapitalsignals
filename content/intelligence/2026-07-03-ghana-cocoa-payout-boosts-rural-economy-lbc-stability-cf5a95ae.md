COCOBOD has released GH¢2.6 billion to Licensed Buying Companies (LBCs) to settle outstanding payments to cocoa farmers. Notably, GH¢1.4 billion of this sum is specifically allocated to clear previous debts owed for supplied cocoa.

This significant cash injection into cocoa communities boosts farmer liquidity, driving potential increases in rural consumption across various sectors. For LBCs, settling these debts improves their financial stability and strengthens the overall cocoa supply chain, reducing operational risks and enhancing future crop security. This positively impacts banks and financial institutions with exposure to the agricultural sector.

Monitor the efficiency of fund disbursement by LBCs and its immediate impact on rural economic activity. We should also track any shifts in farmer sentiment or productivity for the upcoming harvest seasons. Observe COCOBOD's future financing mechanisms and their sustained ability to ensure timely payments.
