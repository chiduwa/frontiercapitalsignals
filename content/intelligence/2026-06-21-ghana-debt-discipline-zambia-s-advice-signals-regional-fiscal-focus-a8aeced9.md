Zambia's Finance Minister, Situmbeko Musokotwane, advised Ghana to implement stricter debt accumulation laws. Zambia itself passed legislation to tie the hands of its executive, granting Parliament greater oversight and control over borrowing.

This advice underscores the region's focus on fiscal discipline, a key concern for international creditors and investors. Ghana, currently under an IMF program, faces immense pressure to demonstrate sustainable public finance management. Stronger legal frameworks for debt can signal improved long term macro stability and reduced sovereign risk.

Monitor Ghana's parliamentary actions and any proposed legislation concerning public debt. Observe how closely Ghana aligns with Zambia's approach, particularly regarding specific spending limits or parliamentary approval mechanisms. Any concrete steps towards enhanced fiscal governance will be a positive signal for investor confidence.
