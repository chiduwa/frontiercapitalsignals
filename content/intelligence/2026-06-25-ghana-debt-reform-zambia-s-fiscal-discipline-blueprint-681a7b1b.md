Zambia's Finance Minister, Situmbeko Musokotwane, advised Ghana to adopt restrictive laws on debt accumulation. He highlighted Zambia's own legislative move to tie the government's hands, giving Parliament more power and imposing limits on future debt.

This counsel matters for investors as it points to a potential move towards greater fiscal conservatism in Ghana. Stronger debt laws could improve macro stability, reduce sovereign risk premiums, and strengthen the cedi over the medium term. This enhances Ghana's long-term attractiveness, even if it implies slower growth in public sector projects initially.

Investors should closely watch the Ghanaian Parliament's reaction and any proposed legislative changes regarding debt ceilings or fiscal responsibility. Observe the IMF's ongoing engagement and the government's commitment to implementing these suggested reforms, as tangible action will drive investor confidence.
