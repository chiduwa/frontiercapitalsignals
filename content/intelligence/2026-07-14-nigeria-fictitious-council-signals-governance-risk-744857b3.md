The Presidential Foreign Intervention Promotion Council (PFIPC), which Nigeria's Presidency has disavowed as fictitious, faces deepening controversy. Its Director-General, Adeyemi, has reportedly received an asylum offer from a US lobbying firm linked to pro-Atiku interests. This development underscores the ongoing dispute over PFIPC's legitimacy and Adeyemi's official standing.

This incident signals persistent governance and regulatory ambiguity in Nigeria. The emergence of entities claiming official ties without clear presidential backing creates an uncertain operating environment. Investors face potential risks including unpredictable policy shifts, reputational damage, and approval delays for projects involving purportedly government-linked initiatives. This emphasizes the critical need for robust due diligence.

Investors should track official clarification on PFIPC's status and any subsequent legal actions against Adeyemi or the organization. Observe the US lobbying firm's continued involvement and the political ramifications of its affiliations, especially as electoral cycles approach. Transparency regarding government-mandated bodies and their authority will be key to assessing investment security.
