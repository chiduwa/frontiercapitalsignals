The Nigerian Senate has approved the Customs Service's 2026 budget of N1.295 trillion, backing an ambitious N11.074 trillion revenue target. This expenditure includes N565 billion specifically allocated for capital projects.

For investors, this signals a significant government focus on maximizing trade-related revenue. The substantial capital expenditure indicates planned modernization and operational upgrades within Customs, potentially impacting logistics efficiency and supply chain costs for import-export businesses. Increased enforcement is likely, creating both challenges and opportunities for companies engaged in cross-border trade.

Watch for details on how Customs plans to achieve its revenue goals, especially the implementation of capital projects and their impact on port operations and border efficiency. Investors should monitor any new tariff structures or enforcement measures that could affect trade flows and associated business profitability in the coming periods.
