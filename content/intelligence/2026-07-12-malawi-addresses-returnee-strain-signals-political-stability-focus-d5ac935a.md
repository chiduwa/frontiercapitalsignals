The SKC Foundation donated K10 million in relief items to Malawian women and children returning home after fleeing xenophobic violence in South Africa.

Large-scale return migration from South Africa, driven by xenophobia, represents a demographic shift and potential strain on Malawi's social services and job market. While direct investment implications are limited, the foundation's involvement highlights political awareness of migration issues, a factor that can influence long-term stability and social cohesion.

Watch for government policies and donor funding addressing returnee integration, particularly regarding employment and housing. Monitor the broader socio-economic impact of sustained return migration on local communities and national budgets, as this pressure could necessitate significant domestic and international support.
