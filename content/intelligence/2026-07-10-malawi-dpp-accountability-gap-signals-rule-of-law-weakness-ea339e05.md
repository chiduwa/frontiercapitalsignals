Parliament in Malawi has confirmed it lacks the constitutional authority to stop the Director of Public Prosecutions (DPP) from dropping criminal cases, even if reasons for doing so are deemed flawed. This admission highlights a significant accountability gap within the country's justice system.

This directly impacts investor confidence and perceptions of the rule of law. Unchecked prosecutorial discretion introduces considerable regulatory and political risk, potentially undermining contract enforcement, fair competition, and the overall business environment. It raises serious concerns about corruption control and the equitable application of justice.

Investors should monitor any legislative or judicial initiatives aiming to address this accountability deficit. Observe specific, high-profile cases where the DPP's discretionary powers are exercised, as these will be key indicators of practical implications for corporate governance and judicial integrity in Malawi.
