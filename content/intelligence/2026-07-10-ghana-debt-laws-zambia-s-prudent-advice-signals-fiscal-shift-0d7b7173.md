Zambia's Minister of Finance, Situmbeko Musokotwane, has formally advised Ghana to implement restrictive laws on debt accumulation. He referenced Zambia's successful legislative approach, which empowered Parliament with greater control and imposed stricter limits on future borrowing.

This counsel is critical for international investors, given Ghana's recent debt challenges and ongoing fiscal restructuring. Adopting similar legislative safeguards could signal a robust commitment to fiscal prudence and debt sustainability, potentially improving sovereign credit ratings and enhancing long-term investor confidence in Ghanaian assets.

Investors should closely track any legislative developments in Ghana, particularly parliamentary debates or ministerial proposals for new fiscal responsibility acts. Monitoring the government's official response to Zambia's advice and any related IMF engagements will be key indicators of Ghana's policy direction.
