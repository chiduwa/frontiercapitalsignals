Zambia's Finance Minister, Situmbeko Musokotwane, publicly advised Ghana to implement restrictive laws on debt accumulation. He cited Zambia's own experience, where a parliamentary law was passed to limit the executive's borrowing powers, preventing past debt accumulation patterns.

This advice underscores a regional shift towards greater fiscal responsibility and transparency. For Ghana, adopting such laws could signal a stronger commitment to debt sustainability, potentially improving credit ratings and reducing future borrowing costs. Investors typically favor markets with clear fiscal frameworks and predictable debt trajectories.

Monitor Ghana's official response and any legislative proposals. Observe whether the International Monetary Fund, or other multilateral lenders, publicly endorse this approach. The successful implementation in Zambia, and its impact on their credit standing, will also serve as a key regional benchmark.
