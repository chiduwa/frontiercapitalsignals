Houthi forces confirmed targeting two Saudi oil tankers, Encelia and Layla, citing a violation of their blockade. This action comes amidst ongoing US attacks on Iran, underscoring rising geopolitical tensions in a critical global shipping corridor.

These attacks elevate geopolitical risk in the Middle East, directly threatening global oil supply chains. For our target markets in Ghana, Nigeria, Kenya, Malawi, and Uganda, sustained instability means potential volatility and upward pressure on crude oil prices, impacting import costs for non-producing nations and potentially boosting revenue for net exporters like Nigeria.

Monitor further US and regional responses, specifically any retaliatory actions or attempts to secure shipping lanes. Watch crude oil benchmarks for price spikes and consider the implications for national budgets. Energy sector companies in the region should prepare for potential cost increases or revenue shifts.
