Kogi and Cross River States are advocating for a transparent, cost-based pricing framework for shared fibre optic duct infrastructure. This initiative aims to facilitate a planned 90,000-kilometer fibre network rollout across Nigeria.

This development signals a critical move towards de-risking telecom infrastructure investments in Nigeria. A clear, predictable pricing model can significantly reduce operational costs for internet service providers and accelerate network expansion, boosting subscriber penetration and enhancing returns for fibre backbone investors. It addresses a major regulatory uncertainty.

Investors should monitor the progression of this advocacy into national policy. Watch for specific regulatory frameworks emerging from the Nigerian Communications Commission and how "cost-based" definitions are established. The speed of implementation and uptake by other states will determine the framework's broader market impact.
