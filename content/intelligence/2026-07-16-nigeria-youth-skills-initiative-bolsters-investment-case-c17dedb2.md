Tomorrow Foundation, in partnership with Thunderbird School of Global Management and Arizona State University, launched a three-year 100 Million Learners Initiative in Nigeria. This program aims to equip Nigerian youth with entrepreneurial and leadership skills vital for the future economy.

This initiative signals a significant push to upskill Nigeria’s massive youth demographic. A more skilled and entrepreneurially driven workforce directly enhances productivity, fosters innovation, and expands the consumer base, creating a more attractive environment for direct and portfolio investment across various sectors.

Monitor the program’s rollout and its integration with local industries. Look for government policies that complement this private sector-led skills development, especially in areas like digital infrastructure and startup incubation. Emerging local ventures or increased foreign direct investment inflows, particularly in tech and service sectors, will be key indicators.
