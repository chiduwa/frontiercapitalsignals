Kenya's Ministry of Transport has confirmed that a transaction advisor has been appointed to structure the $2.1 billion SGR Phase 3 extension from Naivasha to Malaba on the Uganda border, covering 361 kilometers. The government is targeting a hybrid PPP model, blending concessional finance from multilateral development banks with private equity and infrastructure debt. A formal tender is anticipated in Q3 2026, with financial close targeted for mid-2027.

The strategic logic is compelling. Completion of this corridor creates a continuous rail link from Mombasa through Nairobi to Kampala, transforming freight economics for the East African Community's landlocked economies. Current road freight costs on this corridor run approximately $0.12 per tonne-kilometer against a projected rail cost of $0.04, creating immediate demand certainty. Logistics, warehousing, and cold-chain assets proximate to new stations represent strong secondary investment positions alongside direct infrastructure equity.

Investors should engage the Kenya National Highways Authority and Kenya Railways Corporation now. The transaction advisor appointment means legal and financial documentation will move quickly. Priority access to the pre-qualification list will be determined in the next 60 days. This is not a speculative opportunity: the corridor has committed anchor freight customers including a major cement producer and the Kenya Tea Development Agency.
