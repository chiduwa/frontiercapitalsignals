Sierra Leone is hosting the third UN Tourism Africa Gastronomy Forum. This high-profile event is set to convene delegates and stakeholders, showcasing the nation's culinary heritage and potential as a tourism destination.

For investors, this event signifies a broader continent-wide trend: the increasing strategic importance of tourism and cultural diplomacy in driving economic growth. While specifically in Sierra Leone, it underscores Africa's rich, diverse cultural assets as untapped investment opportunities in hospitality, logistics, local food value chains, and tourism infrastructure across countries like Ghana, Kenya, and Nigeria.
