Nigerian consumers are facing significant food inflation. The cost of preparing a pot of jollof rice, a staple, has climbed 14.6% in the last 11 months. This surge is prompting widespread behavioral changes: consumers are purchasing smaller quantities, substituting fresh produce with dried or processed options, and reducing portion sizes.

This trend signals a substantial erosion of consumer purchasing power and will directly impact the Fast-Moving Consumer Goods (FMCG) and retail sectors. Companies focused on premium products, large-format fresh goods, or discretionary food items will likely face headwinds. Opportunities may emerge for businesses offering value-focused, smaller-pack, or shelf-stable alternatives.

Investors should closely monitor companies in the FMCG and food service industries for adaptation strategies, particularly those adjusting product lines to meet budget-constrained demand. We advise tracking broader inflation metrics and government interventions to stabilize food prices, as these will dictate future consumer spending patterns and sector profitability.
