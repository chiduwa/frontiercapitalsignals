Tomorrow Foundation has expanded its 100 Million Learners Initiative in Nigeria. This involves a new three-year partnership with Thunderbird School of Global Management and Arizona State University. The program aims to equip Nigeria's young generation with entrepreneurial and leadership skills for the future economy.

This initiative signals a significant push to develop human capital in Nigeria. A skilled, entrepreneurial workforce drives productivity and innovation, attracting further foreign direct investment. It improves the long-term economic outlook, benefiting sectors from technology to consumer goods, by expanding the talent pool and local market sophistication.

Monitor the initiative's rollout and success metrics, particularly job creation and startup formation rates among participants. Also, observe any government policies or private sector partnerships that leverage this newly developed talent. This will indicate further investment opportunities in Nigeria's burgeoning youth economy.
