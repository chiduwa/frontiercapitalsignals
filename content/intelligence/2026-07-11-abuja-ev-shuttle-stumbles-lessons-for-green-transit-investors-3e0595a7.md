Abuja's new electric airport shuttle, designed to offer a low-emission transport option, is struggling with passenger uptake. Despite its green credentials, limited routes, long waiting times, and low public awareness are hindering its success, and it struggles to compete with established taxi services.

This situation highlights significant operational and market entry challenges for green transport infrastructure projects in Nigeria. Investors considering sustainable urban mobility solutions or last-mile logistics in African emerging markets must account for local competitive landscapes, public awareness, route optimization, and service reliability beyond just the technology itself.

Monitor any strategic adjustments by the shuttle operators, such as route expansion, reduced waiting times, or aggressive marketing campaigns. Observe government support or regulatory changes aimed at incentivizing electric vehicle adoption or reducing traditional taxi dominance, which could signal improved viability for similar green initiatives.
