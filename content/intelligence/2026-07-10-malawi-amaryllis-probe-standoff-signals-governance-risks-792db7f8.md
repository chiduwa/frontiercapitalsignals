A standoff has erupted between the Malawian government and Parliament’s Public Accounts Committee over whether former Secretary to the President and Cabinet Colleen Zamba should testify. This dispute further delays the release of the long-awaited report into the controversial K90 billion Amaryllis Hotel purchase.

This deadlock directly impacts investor confidence by showcasing a lack of transparency and accountability in governance. Prolonged investigations and political interference in oversight bodies raise red flags about the regulatory environment and the rule of law, increasing perceived political risk for large-scale investments.

Investors should monitor developments in the PAC probe, specifically whether Zamba testifies and if the report is ultimately released. The resolution of this dispute will indicate the government's commitment to tackling corruption and upholding accountability, critical for long-term stability.
