Zambia’s Finance Minister, Situmbeko Musokotwane, has publicly advised Ghana to implement restrictive laws on debt accumulation. He referenced Zambia’s own legislative action, where a law was passed to empower Parliament with greater oversight and limitations on government borrowing.

This counsel signals a growing regional imperative for enhanced fiscal discipline among African nations, particularly those grappling with high debt burdens. For Ghana, seriously considering or adopting such legislation could significantly bolster investor confidence by demonstrating a firm commitment to long-term fiscal stability and responsible debt management. This could positively influence sovereign risk perceptions.

Investors should closely monitor Ghana’s official response to this recommendation. Key indicators will include any governmental statements, proposed legislative actions, or parliamentary discussions aimed at introducing new debt ceiling laws or strengthening existing fiscal oversight mechanisms. Watch for alignment with ongoing IMF program conditions.
