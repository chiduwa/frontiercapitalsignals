President Mutharika delegated a cabinet minister to the memorial service for late Vice President Saulos Chilima, despite the sitting Vice President, Jane Ansah, also attending. This decision, now a subject of public debate and opinion pieces, unfolded while investigations into Chilima's fatal plane crash continue.

Such political dynamics, even seemingly minor protocol issues, can signal underlying political friction or governance challenges. Investors prioritize stability, clear lines of authority, and predictable leadership. Public discourse questioning presidential decisions, especially during a national tragedy and ongoing inquiry, adds a layer of political uncertainty, potentially influencing long-term risk assessments for the country.
