Singapore has been named the world's most competitive economy by the IMD World Competitiveness report 2026, outranking Hong Kong and the U.S. This global recognition highlights the effectiveness of its strategic economic planning and robust institutional framework.

For investors in frontier markets, Singapore's success provides a clear model. Its dominance underscores the critical importance of a stable regulatory environment, efficient governance, and sustained investment in infrastructure and human capital. These factors attract and retain significant international capital, driving long term growth.

We advise tracking policy announcements and reform implementations across Ghana, Nigeria, Kenya, Malawi, and Uganda. Focus on initiatives enhancing ease of doing business, digital infrastructure, and skilled labor development, as these will be key indicators of improving competitiveness.
