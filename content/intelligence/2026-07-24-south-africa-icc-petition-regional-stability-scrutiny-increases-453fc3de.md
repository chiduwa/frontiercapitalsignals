Two Ghanaian nationals have petitioned the International Criminal Court, requesting an examination into whether repeated xenophobic attacks in South Africa constitute crimes against humanity. Pretoria has dismissed the filing as opportunistic. Crucially, the ICC has not yet opened an investigation.

While focused on South Africa, this development warrants attention from investors in our focus markets. Persistent international scrutiny over human rights or social stability issues in a major regional economic anchor can ripple across the continent. It impacts broader risk perceptions and could indirectly affect regional trade, supply chains, and investor confidence in proximate emerging markets.

Investors should closely monitor the ICC's official response to the petition, specifically whether it initiates a preliminary examination. Also, watch for any new governmental initiatives from Pretoria to address xenophobia and potential reactions from key international partners or regional bodies. These actions will signal future stability.
