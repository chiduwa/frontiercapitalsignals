Oil prices are posting their strongest weekly gains since April, driven by renewed conflict and a deepening crisis in the Strait of Hormuz. Global crude benchmarks are reacting sharply to the escalating geopolitical risk, signaling sustained upward pressure on energy markets.

For Nigeria, this directly translates to increased export earnings and stronger government revenues, bolstering fiscal stability and potentially strengthening the Naira. However, net oil importers like Kenya, Ghana, and Uganda will face higher import costs, risking inflation and currency depreciation. Investors should prepare for divergent economic impacts across the region.

Continue monitoring the geopolitical developments in the Middle East for any shifts in supply stability. For net importers, watch central bank policy responses to inflation and currency pressures. For Nigeria, track government revenue utilization and foreign exchange reserve accretion closely.
