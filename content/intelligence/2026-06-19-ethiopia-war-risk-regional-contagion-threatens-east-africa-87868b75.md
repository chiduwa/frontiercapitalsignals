Ethiopia faces a significantly heightened risk of a broader, more devastating war. Emerging alliances among anti-government forces and intensified federal militarisation policies are converging, pushing the nation towards a wider conflict.

This escalating internal conflict poses substantial regional contagion risk. Investors should anticipate potential disruptions to regional trade routes, supply chains, and overall market stability, particularly for cross-border investments and East African assets. Capital flight and increased risk premiums across neighboring economies are plausible outcomes.

Monitor the formation and strength of anti-government alliances and the federal government's response. Watch for any cross-border incursions or direct involvement of regional actors. Indicators of humanitarian crises or significant displacement will also signal worsening conditions, further impacting regional stability and investor sentiment.
