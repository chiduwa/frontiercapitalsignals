Africa's digital economy is experiencing significant growth, with the technology sector's contribution to GDP across the continent projected to increase from 5.2% in 2025 to 8.5% by 2030. This rapid expansion is, however, intensifying the challenge for businesses to find adequate IT talent, a critical bottleneck for further development, according to a recent SAP report.

This deepening talent gap presents both a risk and an opportunity for investors. While it could constrain the scalability and profitability of some tech ventures, it simultaneously highlights the premium on companies with robust talent acquisition and retention strategies. Furthermore, it signals significant investment opportunities in education technology, specialized training platforms, and talent outsourcing solutions aimed at closing this critical skills deficit.

Investors should closely monitor government initiatives and private sector partnerships focused on digital skills development and vocational training across key markets. Observe merger and acquisition activity, as securing established talent teams will become a strategic imperative. We will also track the emergence and funding rounds of startups providing innovative solutions to alleviate the IT talent shortage.
