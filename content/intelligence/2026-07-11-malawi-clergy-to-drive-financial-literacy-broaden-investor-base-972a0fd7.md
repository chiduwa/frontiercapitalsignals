MISALICO, with K20 million support from National Bank of Malawi, has launched a financial literacy program targeting clergy. This initiative aims to educate Malawians about the stock market and various investment avenues, significantly broadening financial knowledge nationwide.

This program represents a key driver for deepening Malawi's nascent capital markets. A more financially literate population, particularly with clergy outreach, translates to an expanded domestic investor base and increased participation in listed securities, potentially improving liquidity and valuations for local firms.

Investors should monitor the program's rollout and its impact on the Malawi Stock Exchange. Look for early indicators such as rising retail investor accounts, increased trading volumes, and potential upward pressure on the valuations of well-governed, listed companies.
