Kenya's political class is significantly increasing spending, distributing cash and hosting multimillion-shilling "empowerment" rallies ahead of the 2027 election. This surge in activity comes with growing scrutiny over the opaque sources of these substantial funds.

For investors, this signals potential fiscal indiscipline and heightened governance risks. Large, untraceable cash injections into the economy can fuel inflation, currency volatility, and increased public debt, impacting business operating costs and overall macroeconomic stability.

Moving forward, monitor the government's fiscal policy and the central bank's response to inflationary pressures. Observe any intensified anti-corruption efforts or regulatory actions addressing illicit financial flows, as these will indicate the commitment to financial probity.
