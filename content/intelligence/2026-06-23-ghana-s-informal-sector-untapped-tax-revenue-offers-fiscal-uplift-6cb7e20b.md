Ghana faces a significant fiscal paradox. Its informal sector, employing 80% of the workforce and contributing up to 40% of GDP, generates less than 5% of tax revenue. This vast untaxed economic activity is a key focus for authorities seeking to broaden the tax base.

For investors, this tax gap presents a dual dynamic. Unaddressed, it constrains government finances, potentially impacting bond yields and public service delivery. However, successful policy implementation to capture this revenue could significantly bolster fiscal stability, reduce reliance on external borrowing, and improve the overall operating environment for businesses through enhanced public goods and services.

Investors should monitor Ghana's specific legislative proposals and pilot programs aimed at direct informal sector taxation. Watch for government engagement with business associations and public reception to new tax regimes. Success will be evident in sustained increases in domestic revenue, improved fiscal metrics, and potential shifts in business formalization trends.
