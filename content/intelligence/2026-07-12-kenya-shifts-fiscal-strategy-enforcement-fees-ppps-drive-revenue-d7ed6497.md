Kenya's government is pivoting its fiscal strategy. Facing revenue shortfalls and political resistance to new taxes ahead of the 2027 election, Nairobi will prioritize tougher tax enforcement, increased user fees, and private sector partnerships over broad-based tax hikes.

This shift signals a more targeted approach to revenue generation, potentially reducing the burden of unpredictable widespread tax increases on businesses. Investors should anticipate increased scrutiny on tax compliance and a greater push for public-private partnership (PPP) opportunities, especially in sectors that can generate user fees.

Monitor the specific sectors targeted for user fee increases and enforcement drives. Also, watch for concrete announcements and legislative frameworks supporting new PPP initiatives. The success of this strategy will impact Kenya's fiscal stability and investor confidence in the lead-up to the 2027 election.
