Nigeria's Economic and Financial Crimes Commission, EFCC, has officially cleared Kemi Nandap, the Comptroller General of Immigration, of visa racketeering allegations. This development follows a probe into the service. However, the EFCC confirmed ongoing cases against other officers within the department.

For international investors, this mixed signal is key. Clearing the CG can be seen as a sign of due process and a nuanced approach to investigations, rather than blanket accusations. Yet, the confirmation of cases against other officers underscores persistent corruption risks within vital government agencies. Efficient, transparent immigration processes are crucial for expatriate talent mobility and overall business operations.

Investors should monitor how the EFCC proceeds with prosecuting the implicated officers and any subsequent reforms within the Immigration Service. Watch for new policies aimed at enhancing transparency and efficiency in visa and permit processing. These actions will indicate the true commitment to systemic improvements affecting the ease of doing business in Nigeria.
