Zambia's Finance Minister, Situmbeko Musokotwane, recently advised Ghana to enact restrictive laws on debt accumulation. He highlighted Zambia's own legislative move to tie the minister's hands, granting Parliament greater power and imposing stricter limits on future borrowing. This counsel underscores a regional push for improved fiscal governance.

For investors, the adoption of such a law in Ghana would signal a strong commitment to fiscal discipline and debt sustainability. This could reduce sovereign risk, improve credit ratings over time, and enhance Ghana's attractiveness for long-term capital. It indicates a proactive approach to managing public finances, potentially stabilizing macroeconomic conditions.

Monitor Ghana's official response to this advice and any subsequent legislative proposals. Investors should watch for concrete steps towards parliamentary engagement and the drafting of a specific debt ceiling or fiscal responsibility law. The effectiveness of Zambia's own implemented law in controlling debt will also offer a valuable precedent to observe.
