A Malawi court in Machinga acquitted a man accused of assaulting a 96-year-old woman, Maggie Tenesi, over witchcraft allegations following a child's death. This ruling has sparked significant public outrage and disappointment among local communities.

This incident signals potential fragilities within Malawi's justice system and the broader rule of law, particularly when traditional beliefs intersect with legal processes. A perceived unreliable or unjust legal framework increases operational risk for investors, impacts contract enforceability, and can undermine public trust, which is crucial for long-term stability and investment security.

We need to monitor public reactions and any official government or judicial responses. Observe whether authorities address the public's concerns regarding judicial fairness and protection of vulnerable populations. Any moves towards strengthening judicial independence and transparency will be a key indicator for investor confidence.
