Malawi's Human Rights Consultative Committee and other civil society groups are publicly demanding the government revoke the citizenship of Pakistani-born businessman Mahmood Azhar Chaudhry. They are also calling for his arrest and deportation, citing authorities' inability to prosecute him.

This development signals increased regulatory scrutiny and potential political risk for foreign investors in Malawi. The lack of clear legal resolution, coupled with public pressure for extreme measures like citizenship revocation, highlights potential unpredictability in the business environment and risks to property rights or personal security for expatriate entrepreneurs.

Monitor the government's official response to these demands. Observe any legal proceedings initiated against Chaudhry or his businesses. This case will be a key indicator of Malawi's commitment to rule of law, fair due process, and its broader approach to attracting and protecting foreign direct investment.
