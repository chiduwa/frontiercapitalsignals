Malawi is facing significant repatriation of its undocumented nationals from South Africa, sparking debate over government accountability. This ongoing situation highlights deep-seated issues including economic migration, intensified immigration enforcement, and South African labour market pressures.

This trend directly impacts Malawi's economic stability, particularly through reduced remittances, a vital source of foreign exchange and household income. Large-scale returns also strain domestic social services and the labour market, potentially dampening consumer confidence and increasing fiscal pressures on the Malawian government.

Monitor the Malawian government's response to integrate returning citizens and any bilateral agreements with South Africa on migration policy. We should also watch for shifts in remittance inflows and local employment figures, as these will indicate the short to medium term economic impact.
