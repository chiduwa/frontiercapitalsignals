South African startup Refreshi has demonstrated significant market traction, expanding its surplus-food marketplace from 35 to 700 partner stores in one year. This platform prevented 200,000 meals from landfill, saving consumers R12.5 million on groceries, as detailed in its first annual Grocery Savings Impact Report.

This success highlights a scalable and impactful business model for investors. Refreshi's rapid growth and tangible economic and environmental benefits showcase strong market demand for tech-driven solutions addressing food waste in emerging markets. Such ventures offer attractive returns alongside strong ESG credentials, a combination increasingly sought by international funds exploring Africa.

We should monitor Refreshi's potential for pan-African expansion or the emergence of similar ventures in Ghana, Nigeria, Kenya, Malawi, or Uganda. Also, watch for government policies or incentives supporting food waste reduction across these regions, as they could create an even more favorable environment for investment in this growing sector.
