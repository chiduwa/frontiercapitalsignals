The Strait of Hormuz has reopened, easing immediate pressure on global fertilizer markets. However, this crisis highlighted the vulnerability of major players like Morocco’s OCP, revealing its significant reliance on imported sulphur and ammonia for production.

This exposure signals potential volatility in global fertilizer input prices, directly impacting the operational costs and profitability of agricultural companies across Africa. Investors should assess ripple effects on food security and agricultural yields in Ghana, Nigeria, Kenya, Malawi, and Uganda.

Watch for OCP’s strategic response to diversify input sourcing or localize production, which could influence future global supply stability. Monitor how African nations respond to potential price shifts, assessing opportunities in local fertilizer production or alternative agricultural input development.
