Zambia's Finance Minister, Situmbeko Musokotwane, has publicly advised Ghana to adopt restrictive laws on debt accumulation. He highlighted Zambia's strategy of passing legislation that limits government borrowing, empowering Parliament with greater oversight and control.

This advice is critical for investors, signalling a potential shift towards enhanced fiscal discipline in Ghana. Implementing such laws could improve Ghana's credit profile, reduce sovereign risk, and attract more long term capital by ensuring a more sustainable debt trajectory.

Investors should closely monitor Ghana's response. Watch for any legislative initiatives, public statements from the Ministry of Finance, or parliamentary discussions signaling a commitment to stricter debt management. The actual drafting and implementation of such laws will be crucial.
