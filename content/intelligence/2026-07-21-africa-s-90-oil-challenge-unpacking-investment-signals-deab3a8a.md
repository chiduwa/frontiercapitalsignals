Global oil prices have climbed above $90 a barrel due to escalating US-Iran hostilities, immediately threatening African economies. This surge is set to fuel mounting inflation and widen budget deficits across the continent, straining fiscal stability and consumer purchasing power.

For investors, this means increased risk for import-dependent sectors and sovereign debt. However, it also highlights strategic plays in energy independence, such as Dangote’s refining capacity in Nigeria. Government measures like tax cuts or fuel stockpiling, aimed at mitigating impact, could also create niche opportunities.

Monitor key African oil producers for potential revenue windfalls versus consumer nations' inflation data and subsidy policies. Watch specific government responses, currency stability, and the operational timelines of domestic refining projects. Energy sector agility and fiscal discipline remain critical investment watch points.
