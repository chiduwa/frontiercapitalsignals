The Centre for Democracy and Economic Development Initiatives (CDEDI) has threatened street protests in Malawi over a public university tuition fee hike. The government, through its minister, has explicitly ruled out reversing the decision, setting the stage for a direct confrontation between civil society and the state.

For investors, this signals rising social and political risk. Widespread protests could disrupt commerce, impact business operations, and deter foreign direct investment. It also highlights the government's fiscal tightening measures, which, while necessary for budget stability, carry the risk of public backlash and instability.

Monitor the scale and duration of any protests. Key indicators include government's response, potential for escalation, and any spillover effects into other sectors or regions. This situation will test social cohesion and the government's capacity to manage public dissent without broader economic disruption.
