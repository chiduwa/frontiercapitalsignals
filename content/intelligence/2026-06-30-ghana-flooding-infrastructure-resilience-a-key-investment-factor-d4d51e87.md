Accra experienced significant disruption this Monday due to torrential rainfall. Widespread flooding paralyzed business activity, halted transport, and forced school closures. Several communities, including Dzorwulu, Mallam, and Kasoa, lost electricity as floodwaters submerged roads and homes across the capital.

This event highlights critical infrastructure vulnerabilities in Ghana, posing immediate operational risks for businesses. Supply chain disruptions, increased logistics costs, and potential for prolonged power outages directly impact corporate earnings and business continuity. Investors must factor in these recurring climate-related risks when assessing operational stability and long-term growth potential for assets in Accra.

Monitor the government's response and any accelerated plans for flood mitigation and infrastructure upgrades, particularly in drainage and power grid resilience. Observe the short-term economic impact on affected sectors like retail, logistics, and utilities. Further, track the effectiveness of disaster preparedness and recovery efforts as indicators of future operational stability for companies operating in the capital.
