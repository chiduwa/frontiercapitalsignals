Malawi's government has failed to operationalize the Real Estate Management Act, 18 months after Parliament passed it. This regulatory inaction leaves house hunters exposed to widespread property scams, including fake listings and fraudulent deposits, as rogue agents exploit the country's acute housing shortage.

This regulatory vacuum creates significant market risk within Malawi's real estate sector. The absence of oversight deters legitimate foreign direct investment, inflates due diligence costs, and increases the potential for legal disputes and reputational damage for any stakeholder. It underscores broader governance challenges impacting market predictability and investor confidence.

Monitor government announcements or concrete steps to activate the Real Estate Management Act and establish its regulator. Watch for private sector advocacy or industry association pressure pushing for implementation. Also, observe any high-profile legal actions by scam victims, which could force government action.
