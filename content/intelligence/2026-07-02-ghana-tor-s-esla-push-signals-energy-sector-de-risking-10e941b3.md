Tema Oil Refinery, TOR, management initiated direct talks with the Ghanaian government to recapitalize the refinery. A key proposal involves releasing a minimum GH¢1.6 billion in outstanding Energy Sector Levy Act, ESLA, receivables.

This development is crucial for addressing TOR's persistent financial challenges. Releasing these funds could significantly strengthen its balance sheet, potentially reducing government's contingent liabilities and enhancing broader energy sector stability. It might also signal opportunities for future private investment.

Monitor the government's official response and any definitive timelines for ESLA fund disbursement. Also, watch for clarity on TOR's operational turnaround plan and the broader implications for Ghana's energy sector debt framework.
