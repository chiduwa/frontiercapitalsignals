President Mutharika has directed his new Mining Minister, Thoko Tembo, to pursue significantly tougher mineral deals. He cited past failures from poor negotiation skills and cartels, stressing the need for Malawi to gain more from its mineral wealth.

This signals a potential shift towards stricter regulatory oversight and more demanding terms for new or renegotiated mining concessions. Investors in Malawi's mineral sector should anticipate increased scrutiny on deal structures, profit-sharing, and local content. While aiming for national benefit, this could impact investor returns.

Watch for the new Minister's initial policy statements and any announced reviews of current mining agreements. Specifics of new negotiation frameworks and government action against cartels will clarify the policy change and operational impacts for mining companies.
