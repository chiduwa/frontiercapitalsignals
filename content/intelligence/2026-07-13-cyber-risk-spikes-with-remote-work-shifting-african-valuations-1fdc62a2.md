The modern workplace has expanded far beyond traditional offices, now encompassing diverse locations from coffee shops to co-working spaces. This shift to hybrid and remote models, while boosting flexibility, has critically increased organisations' digital attack surface, significantly escalating cyber security vulnerabilities.

For investors in African markets, this dynamic demands a re-evaluation of portfolio companies. Businesses that fail to adequately invest in robust cybersecurity will face heightened operational risks, potential data breaches, and reputational damage, impacting valuations and investor confidence. Conversely, strong digital resilience signals maturity and risk mitigation.

Moving forward, investors should closely monitor regulatory developments in data protection and cyber compliance across Ghana, Nigeria, Kenya, Malawi, and Uganda. Observe corporate IT spending trends, especially increased allocations to security infrastructure and employee training. The emergence and scaling of local cybersecurity solution providers also present compelling investment opportunities.
