Nigeria's opposition, the African Democratic Congress (ADC), is demanding a full breakdown of N7.13 trillion recorded as "energy security" expenditure.

This demand highlights growing political scrutiny over significant public spending in Nigeria’s critical energy sector. For investors, it signals potential governance risks and questions around the efficiency and allocation of substantial funds intended for energy stability.

Watch for the government's response and any subsequent disclosures regarding these funds. The outcome will set a precedent for transparency in future large-scale government energy initiatives and could influence investor confidence in Nigeria's energy reforms.
