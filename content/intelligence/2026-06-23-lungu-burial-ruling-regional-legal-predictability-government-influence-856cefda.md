South Africa's Supreme Court of Appeal ruled that former Zambian President Edgar Lungu will be buried in South Africa, overturning an earlier judgment. This decision upholds his family's wishes, preventing the Zambian government from repatriating his remains for a state funeral in Lusaka.

This ruling, while specific, signals potential inter-state legal complexities and the assertion of individual or family rights against government influence in politically sensitive cases. For investors, it underscores the importance of legal predictability, the robustness of judicial systems, and the separation of powers across Southern Africa. It highlights governance narratives around governmental authority.
