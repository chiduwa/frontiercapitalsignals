Malawi's Minister of Transport and Public Works, Jappie Mhango, officially launched the rehabilitation of the 47-kilometre Matawale–Liwonde Road. This groundbreaking ceremony in Zomba District reaffirms the government’s stated commitment to enhancing nationwide road infrastructure.

For investors, this project signifies improved logistics and reduced operational costs for businesses reliant on efficient transport. Enhanced connectivity typically stimulates regional trade, agriculture, and tourism, creating new market opportunities. It also signals a reliable pipeline of government-backed infrastructure projects, a positive indicator for construction and related service sectors.
