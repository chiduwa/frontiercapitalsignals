Industrialist Kow Sam argues that Accra’s persistent flooding and escalating economic losses demand a strategic shift. He proposes relocating Ghana’s administrative capital to the Accra-Kumasi expressway corridor to mitigate climate risk, framing it as essential to avoid an economic 'time bomb'.

This isn't merely a policy debate, it's a potential catalyst for massive infrastructure investment. A capital relocation would trigger significant spending on urban development, transport networks, utilities, and real estate. Investors should consider opportunities in land acquisition, construction, logistics, and smart city solutions along the proposed corridor.

Watch for government engagement with this proposal, particularly any official feasibility studies or task forces. Monitor public and private sector reactions, and look for any preliminary steps towards planning or budgeting. This debate will highlight political will and financial appetite for climate adaptation and large-scale development.
