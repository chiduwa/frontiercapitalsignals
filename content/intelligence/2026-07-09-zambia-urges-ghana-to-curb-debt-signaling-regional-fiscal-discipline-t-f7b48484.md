Zambia's Finance Minister Situmbeko Musokotwane has advised Ghana to implement restrictive laws on debt accumulation. He referenced Zambia's own legislative move to empower Parliament with greater control and limits on government borrowing, a measure designed to prevent a return to unsustainable debt practices.

This recommendation underscores a growing regional emphasis on fiscal discipline, crucial for attracting and retaining investor confidence. For Ghana, adopting similar stringent debt laws could significantly bolster its commitment to long term debt sustainability, potentially leading to improved credit ratings and lower sovereign borrowing costs. It signals a shift towards more predictable and accountable economic governance.

Investors should closely monitor Ghana's official response and any subsequent legislative actions taken regarding new debt management frameworks. Also, observe the practical impact of Zambia's own restrictive laws on its fiscal health and investor perception, as its experience could offer a blueprint or cautionary example for Ghana and other peer markets.
