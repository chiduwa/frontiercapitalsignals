Malawi’s cotton industry has suffered a dramatic 94% collapse in production, dropping from over 100,000 metric tonnes to approximately 6,000. This severe decline leaves thousands of farmers and dependent businesses in an uncertain future, marking the industry’s lowest output in years.

This crisis signals significant systemic risk within Malawi's agricultural sector, a core economic pillar. Investors should note potential impacts on rural purchasing power, national export earnings, and the stability of the Malawian Kwacha. Businesses reliant on cotton, such as ginning and textile operations, face severe supply chain disruptions and potential operational closures.

Monitor the Malawian government’s response, including any support packages or diversification strategies for affected farmers. Observe the identified root causes of this collapse, whether climate, policy, or market driven, as these will inform future risk assessments across other agricultural investments in the region. Track any ripple effects on related sectors, particularly finance and logistics.
