Airtel Africa cut 9.1 million litres of diesel in FY2025/2026, advancing its green initiatives across 14 African markets. This significant reduction reflects a strategic shift towards cleaner operations and a commitment to sustainability while pursuing business growth.

This move demonstrates tangible progress in operational efficiency and ESG commitment, potentially lowering long-term operating costs and enhancing investor appeal. Reduced reliance on diesel hedges against fuel price volatility, improving margin predictability in key growth markets like Ghana, Nigeria, Kenya, Malawi, and Uganda. Such sustainability efforts can also attract impact investors and meet evolving regulatory standards.
