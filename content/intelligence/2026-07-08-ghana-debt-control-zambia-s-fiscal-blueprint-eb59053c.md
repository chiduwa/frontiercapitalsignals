Zambia's Finance Minister, Situmbeko Musokotwane, has formally advised Ghana to implement restrictive laws on government debt accumulation. He highlighted Zambia's own legislative action, which empowered Parliament with greater oversight and limitations on the finance minister's ability to incur new debt.

For investors, this counsel underscores a growing regional commitment to fiscal discipline and debt sustainability. Should Ghana adopt similar legislative measures, it would signal enhanced macroeconomic stability and reduced sovereign risk, potentially improving creditworthiness and attracting more capital. This move could create a more predictable operating environment.

We are watching for Ghana's response and any legislative proposals stemming from this advice. Specifically, observe parliamentary debates or drafting of new laws aimed at strengthening debt control and fiscal responsibility. Also monitor the tangible impact of Zambia's own restrictive debt law on its fiscal performance.
