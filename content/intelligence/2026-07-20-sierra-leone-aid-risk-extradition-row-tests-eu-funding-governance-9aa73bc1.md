The Netherlands is urging the European Union to consider withholding €352 million in development grants from Sierra Leone. This pressure stems from Sierra Leone's delay in extraditing convicted Dutch drug trafficker Jos Leijdekkers, as reported by the Financial Times.

While focused on Sierra Leone, this situation is a critical signal for investors across frontier markets. International disputes that jeopardize significant grant funding can indicate heightened sovereign risk, governance fragilities, and an unpredictable operating environment. Such actions impact a country's financial stability and its ability to undertake development projects that often underpin private sector growth.
