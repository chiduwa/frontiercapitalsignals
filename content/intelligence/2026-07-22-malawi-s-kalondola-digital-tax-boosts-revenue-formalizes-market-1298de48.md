Malawi's Kalondola digital tax stamp system, after two years, has delivered billions in new excise tax revenue, significantly improving product tracking and market transparency. While effective, the system has not fully eradicated counterfeit goods or the black market.

This signals improved fiscal health for Malawi and a more level playing field for legitimate businesses through reduced competition from untaxed goods. Enhanced product tracking also offers greater market visibility, potentially improving supply chain integrity for investors in consumer goods and manufacturing.

Monitor MRA's continued efforts and strategies to curb the black market and counterfeit goods, as these will directly impact the growth trajectory of compliant businesses. Observe any expansion of Kalondola to other product categories, signalling broader formalization efforts.
