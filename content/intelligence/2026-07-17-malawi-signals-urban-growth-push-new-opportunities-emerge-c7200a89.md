Malawi's Minister of Lands, Housing and Urban Development, Chimwemwe Chipungu, recently stressed the urgent need for "decisive implementation" regarding urban growth at a UN General Assembly meeting. This statement marks ten years since the New Urban Agenda's adoption, indicating a renewed focus on practical action.

For investors, this signals a potential acceleration of urban development projects. Decisive implementation often translates into increased government spending, public-private partnership opportunities, and tenders in housing, infrastructure, utilities, and construction materials. It suggests a more committed policy environment for tackling urbanisation challenges.

Monitor upcoming policy announcements, specific budget allocations, and major infrastructure project tenders. Look for legislative changes that streamline urban planning and land acquisition, and identify which Malawian cities or regions will be prioritised for development initiatives.
