Prime Minister Abiy Ahmed has initiated a National Dialogue in Ethiopia, ostensibly to strengthen democracy with a two-term limit. However, the conversation has quickly shifted towards a potential constitutional change, transitioning Ethiopia to a presidential republic. This move could reset political clocks, offering Abiy a fresh mandate beyond existing term limits.

This development introduces significant political uncertainty for investors in Ethiopia and the broader Horn of Africa. While a clear long-term political trajectory could offer stability, the current debate signals potential for leadership consolidation or a constitutional re-engineering that could alter the country's governance structure. Such shifts often lead to policy unpredictability, affecting investment climate, regulatory stability, and long-term project viability.

Monitor the National Dialogue closely for concrete proposals on constitutional amendments, especially regarding the shift to a presidential system and how term limits would apply under such a change. Observe domestic and international reactions to these proposals. The level of consensus or opposition will indicate the potential for political stability or increased internal friction, key factors for sustained investor confidence.
