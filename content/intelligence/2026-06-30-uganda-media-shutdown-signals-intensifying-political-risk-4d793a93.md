Uganda's military chief, General Muhoozi Kainerugaba, ordered the immediate shutdown of leading independent media outlets, Daily Monitor and NTV Uganda. Soldiers were deployed to the Nation Media Group offices in Kampala, enforcing the directive.

This move significantly raises concerns about military overreach and the erosion of civilian governance, critical factors for investor confidence. Such actions signal increasing political risk, potential instability, and an unpredictable operating environment. It implies a reduced commitment to democratic norms, which often correlates with policy inconsistency and rule of law challenges.

Investors should monitor the duration of the shutdown and any official justifications provided. Observe international reactions, especially from key development partners. Also watch for broader government responses to dissent or any escalation of security forces' involvement in civilian affairs, as this will further define the political risk landscape.
