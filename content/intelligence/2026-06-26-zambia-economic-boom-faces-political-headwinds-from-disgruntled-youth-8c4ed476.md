President Hichilema's administration in Zambia showcases significant economic achievements, notably a robust mining sector and strong agricultural output. However, this success is increasingly overshadowed by growing youth dissatisfaction over stalled democratic reforms.

For investors, this presents a complex risk-reward profile. While solid economic fundamentals underpin growth, particularly in primary sectors, the youth's disillusionment signals potential for political instability and social unrest, especially as elections approach. This dynamic can impact policy predictability and long-term project viability.

Monitor the government's actions regarding youth grievances and progress on democratic reform pledges. Observe pre-election political rhetoric and public sentiment indicators closely, as these will signal the likelihood of sustained stability or increased volatility. Pay attention to any shifts in mining or agricultural policy potentially aimed at appeasing discontent.
