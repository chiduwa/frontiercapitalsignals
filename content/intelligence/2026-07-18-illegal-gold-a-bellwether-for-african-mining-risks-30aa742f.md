Cameroon is losing billions due to widespread illegal gold mining, driven by porous borders and high-level complicity. Despite abundant resources, the state captures minimal revenue, with mining contributing barely 1% to national GDP.

For international investors, this situation underscores critical systemic risks across resource-rich African nations. It signals potential for revenue leakage, an uneven playing field, and operational challenges for legitimate formal sector investments in environments with weak regulatory enforcement. This strengthens the investment case for countries like Ghana or Uganda, which offer comparatively more transparent and robust governance frameworks within the mining sector.

Investors should closely watch for governmental responses, any announced reforms to formalize artisanal mining, and efforts to curb illicit trade. Further investigative reports could reveal specific actors or mechanisms, providing crucial insights for future risk assessments of resource investments across the continent.
