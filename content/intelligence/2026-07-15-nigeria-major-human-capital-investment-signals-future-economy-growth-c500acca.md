Tomorrow Foundation, in partnership with Thunderbird School of Global Management and Arizona State University, launched a three-year 100 Million Learners Initiative in Nigeria. This program aims to equip Nigerian youth with entrepreneurial and leadership skills for the future economy.

This initiative directly addresses the critical skills gap, fostering a more capable workforce and future consumer base. It signals growing human capital investment, enhancing long-term economic stability and attracting foreign direct investment into Nigeria's tech and innovation sectors.

Monitor the program's implementation and its impact on specific sectors like digital services and e-learning. Also, observe any complementary government policies or private sector collaborations that emerge to leverage this skilled talent pool.
