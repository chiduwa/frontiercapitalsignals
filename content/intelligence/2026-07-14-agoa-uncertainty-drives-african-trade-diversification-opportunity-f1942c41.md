AGOA's recent one-year renewal introduces significant uncertainty for African exporters. The incoming US administration signals a shift towards demanding reciprocity in trade relations, challenging the traditional one-way preference system of AGOA.

This forces African nations to accelerate trade diversification beyond US markets. Investors should prioritize businesses and sectors demonstrating strong regional trade connections, robust domestic demand, or established export channels to non-Western economies. Focus on value chains resilient to potential AGOA disruptions.

Key areas to watch include new bilateral trade agreements pursued by African countries with non-US partners, especially China, EU, and Gulf nations. Also, observe domestic policy reforms aimed at strengthening internal markets and progress within the African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA), which offers a significant hedge against US policy shifts.
