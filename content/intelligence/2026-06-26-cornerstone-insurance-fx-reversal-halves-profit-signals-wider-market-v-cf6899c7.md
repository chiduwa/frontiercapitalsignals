Cornerstone Insurance reported a 54.6 percent decline in 2025 post-tax profit. This sharp drop occurred as net foreign exchange gains, a substantial profit driver in 2024 at N30.8 billion, inverted to a N6.1 billion negative position.

This outcome demonstrates the significant impact of currency volatility on Nigerian corporate earnings, especially for companies with material FX exposure. For investors, it underscores the need to differentiate between sustainable operational profits and one-off or transient FX-driven gains. Reliance on such gains creates inherent earnings instability.

Investors should closely watch Cornerstone's strategies to stabilize core underwriting profitability and manage foreign exchange risk going forward. Furthermore, monitor the Central Bank of Nigeria's broader FX policy direction, as any policy shifts or Naira stabilization will directly influence the earnings outlook for Nigerian listed entities.
