The World Bank is under pressure from Washington to reprioritize growth and energy security over strict climate goals. This signals a potential backtrack on its 45% climate finance target and previous restrictions on funding gas projects.

This shift could unlock new investment opportunities, particularly in gas and other traditional energy infrastructure across Africa. While concessional climate finance might decrease, a renewed focus on energy security supports projects essential for industrial growth and reliable power, attracting diverse private capital that aligns with national development agendas. Investors should re-evaluate energy sector exposure.
