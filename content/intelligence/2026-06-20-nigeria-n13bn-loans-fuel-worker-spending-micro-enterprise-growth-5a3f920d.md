Nigeria's government disbursed N13 billion in interest-free loans to 7,450 tertiary institution workers. Loans, up to N10 million and capped at 33.3% of annual salary, target transportation, healthcare, and small business.

This direct liquidity injection into Nigeria's middle-income segment is significant. It will likely stimulate consumer spending across retail, transportation, and healthcare. Funds for small-scale ventures also support nascent SMEs, contributing to broader economic resilience and potential job creation.

Investors should monitor the immediate impact on consumer confidence and retail sales, alongside any inflationary pressures from increased liquidity. Also, track the success rate of small businesses funded and the overall loan repayment performance, indicators of sustained economic activity.
