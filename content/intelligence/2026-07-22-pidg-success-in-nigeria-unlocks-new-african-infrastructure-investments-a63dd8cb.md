PIDG recently completed a successful $26 million exit from InfraCredit Nigeria, demonstrating the viability of mobilizing local capital for critical infrastructure. Following this success, Philippe Valahu plans to establish up to ten new guarantee platforms across Africa by 2030.

This development is significant for investors. The InfraCredit model validates local currency infrastructure investment, mitigating foreign exchange risks for long-term projects. These new guarantee platforms will de-risk further opportunities, making them more attractive for international institutional capital seeking stable, long-duration returns in key African markets.

Watch closely for announcements regarding the specific countries and sectors targeted by these upcoming guarantee platforms. Identify early opportunities for co-investment or partnership as these new structures come online, particularly within Ghana, Nigeria, Kenya, Malawi, and Uganda.
