Zambia's Finance Minister, Situmbeko Musokotwane, advised Ghana to enact restrictive debt accumulation laws. He highlighted Zambia's own legislative model, which grants Parliament greater oversight and ties the executive's hands on borrowing.

This is critical for investors. Ghana's recent debt struggles and IMF program underscore the need for fiscal discipline. Adopting such laws would signal a strong government commitment to reducing debt risk, potentially improving credit ratings, and enhancing long-term economic stability and predictability for foreign capital.

Watch for any concrete legislative proposals emerging from Accra related to debt ceilings or enhanced parliamentary control over borrowing. Also, observe the IMF's stance and public statements from Ghanaian officials on fiscal reforms and debt management strategies.
