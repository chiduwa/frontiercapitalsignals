Malawi is experiencing a sharp rise in gender-based violence (GBV) cases, reaching record highs. The Parliamentary Women’s Caucus reported cases climbing to 24,900 in 2024 and 24,467 in 2023, significantly up from 20,827 in 2022. Female legislators are now pressing for urgent government action to address this growing crisis.

This trend presents a material risk to Malawi's human capital and economic productivity. High GBV prevalence reduces female labor force participation, increases healthcare burdens, and strains social services, directly impacting GDP growth potential and overall stability. It also signals governance weaknesses and raises significant ESG concerns for investors, potentially affecting long term business operations and social license.

Monitor the government's specific policy responses and budgetary commitments to tackle GBV. Look for concrete legislative reforms, enforcement measures, and the effectiveness of civil society partnerships. Any significant improvement or deterioration in these social indicators will influence investor perceptions of Malawi's stability and long-term development trajectory.
