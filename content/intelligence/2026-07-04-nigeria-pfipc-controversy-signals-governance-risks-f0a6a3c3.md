Fresh official documents contradict the Nigerian presidency's previous denial of the Presidential Foreign Investment Promotion Council (PFIPC)'s existence. This revelation suggests a significant lack of transparency or misrepresentation regarding government agencies operating within the foreign investment landscape.

This incident directly erodes investor confidence by questioning the reliability of official government statements and the integrity of regulatory frameworks. Such discrepancies introduce unpredictable risks, complicating due diligence and increasing the perception of a less stable, less transparent operating environment for foreign capital.

Monitor the government's official response to these findings. We will be looking for immediate corrective actions, any internal investigations, or clear policy statements to restore trust. Further obfuscation or lack of accountability will exacerbate concerns about governance and its impact on the long-term investment climate.
