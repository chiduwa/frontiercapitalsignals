Sylvester Namiwa, Executive Director of CDEDI, publicly alleges First Lady Gertrude Mutharika is behind a growing rift between President Peter Mutharika and Vice-President Jane Ansah. Namiwa remains defiant, stating he fears no intimidation for these claims.

This public accusation of a presidential family interference in high-level political relationships points to deepening internal instability within Malawi's leadership. Such a rift can undermine policy consistency, introduce governance uncertainty, and elevate political risk for investors operating or considering the market.

Monitor the President's response to these allegations and any potential official action against Namiwa. Observe if the apparent rift between Mutharika and Ansah escalates or becomes more overt. This situation may influence governance stability and future political alignments ahead of any upcoming electoral cycles.
