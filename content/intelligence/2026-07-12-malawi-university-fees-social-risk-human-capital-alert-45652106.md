Malawi's government has significantly raised public university tuition fees, a move drawing parliamentary scrutiny and public concern that low-income students will be priced out of higher education. The Minister of Education defended the increase as necessary amidst growing criticism from lawmakers and the public.

This policy shift poses risks to social stability and long-term human capital development. Excluding a significant portion of the population from higher education could lead to social unrest, dampen future consumer spending, and slow the growth of a skilled workforce crucial for business expansion and FDI attractiveness.

Investors should monitor public protests, parliamentary debates, and any government concessions on fee structures. Watch for shifts in social sentiment and potential long-term impacts on the availability of skilled labor, which could influence future operational costs and investment viability in Malawi.
