UN aid operations in El-Obeid, western Sudan, have been suspended. This leaves 600,000 people vulnerable to an imminent Rapid Support Forces (RSF) offensive. The RSF aims to cement its control over the region.

This escalating humanitarian crisis and potential for prolonged conflict in Sudan exacerbates regional instability. It contributes to negative investor sentiment across East Africa, particularly concerning supply chain disruptions and refugee flows. While not a core market for us, the wider perception of risk impacts capital allocation decisions throughout the continent.

Monitor developments regarding any ceasefire agreements or international intervention efforts. We are tracking commodity price volatility, especially for regional agricultural exports, and the potential for increased refugee pressure on neighboring economies. Any signs of the conflict de-escalating, or conversely, spreading further, will require reassessment.
