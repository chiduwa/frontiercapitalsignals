The $15bn Abidjan-Lagos Highway project is accelerating towards construction. ALCoMA, the authority established to deliver the corridor, has appointed its inaugural chairman and approved new project steps. The African Development Bank is also strengthening its financing role as lead arranger for this critical regional infrastructure.

This development signals tangible progress on a project designed to significantly reduce transport costs and deepen trade integration. Investors should note the upcoming economic ripple effects across Ghana, Nigeria, and other West African economies, impacting logistics, manufacturing, real estate, and consumer markets by improving connectivity and market access.

Watch closely for specific financing milestones and the issuance of construction tenders. Identifying key companies poised to benefit directly from contracts, supply chain opportunities, and increased economic activity along the highway route will be crucial. Further details on local content requirements and specific development zones will also be important indicators.
