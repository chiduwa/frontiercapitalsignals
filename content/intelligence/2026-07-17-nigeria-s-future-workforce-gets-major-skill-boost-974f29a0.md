Tomorrow Foundation, in partnership with Thunderbird School of Global Management and Arizona State University, launched a three-year 100 Million Learners Initiative in Nigeria. This program aims to equip Nigerian youth with critical entrepreneurial and leadership skills for the future economy.

This initiative directly addresses a key barrier to growth in Nigeria: human capital development and skill gaps. Investing in education and entrepreneurship creates a more skilled workforce, fostering innovation, boosting local businesses, and expanding the consumer base. This translates to a more dynamic and attractive market for foreign direct investment across various sectors.

Monitor the initiative's rollout and early impact metrics. Look for signs of increased local startup activity, improved employment rates among program participants, and government support for similar skill-building programs. These indicators will signal further maturation of Nigeria's human capital and potential for economic acceleration.
