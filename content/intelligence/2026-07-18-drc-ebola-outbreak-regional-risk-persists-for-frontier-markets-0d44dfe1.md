The Democratic Republic of Congo continues to battle an active Ebola, or BVD, outbreak. As of July 15, 2026, the country has reported a cumulative 2,124 confirmed cases, including 828 fatalities.

While the outbreak remains within DRC borders, its persistence poses a regional contagion risk and could dampen investor sentiment across broader Central and East African markets. Investors monitoring Ghana, Nigeria, Kenya, Malawi, and Uganda should consider potential ripple effects on regional trade, travel, and supply chains, alongside an increased perception of health-related operational risks.

Monitor DRC's containment efforts and any signs of cross-border spread to neighboring states. Track the effectiveness of international health aid and its impact on regional stability. We will be watching for shifts in investor appetite for the wider African frontier market, particularly in sectors sensitive to public health crises or supply chain disruptions.
