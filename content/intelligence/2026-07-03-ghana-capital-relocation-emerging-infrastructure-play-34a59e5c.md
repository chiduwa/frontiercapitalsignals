Ghanaian industrialist Kow Sam, a sustainability specialist, is advocating for relocating Ghana's administrative capital from Accra. He argues Accra's recurrent floods and escalating climate risks are creating an "economic time bomb," incurring billions in losses. Sam suggests the Accra-Kumasi Expressway Corridor as a viable alternative for a new capital city.

This proposal, from a credible voice in industry and climate strategy, signals a growing national recognition of Accra's infrastructure vulnerabilities to climate change. Should this concept gain traction, it would unlock significant long-term infrastructure development, land appreciation, and real estate opportunities within the designated new corridor, shifting investment focus.

Investors should closely monitor government reactions, any official feasibility studies, or the establishment of expert panels to assess capital relocation. Observe land acquisition patterns and early-stage development proposals along the Accra-Kumasi corridor. Assess political will and funding commitment for this potentially transformative national project.
