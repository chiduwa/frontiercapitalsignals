A Nigerian Federal High Court in Kano recently rejected a request to compel a PREMIUM TIMES reporter to disclose sources regarding a port ownership report. The court affirmed a journalist's right to protect their sources, ruling disclosure is only mandated if sources are directly linked to the specific case.

This ruling signals a crucial protection for press freedom and judicial independence in Nigeria. For international investors, stronger protections for investigative journalism translate to enhanced transparency and accountability, particularly concerning public sector projects, governance, and corruption. This development can reduce information asymmetry and systemic risks, fostering a more predictable investment environment.

Investors should monitor subsequent court decisions regarding media freedom and whistleblower protections. Observe how this precedent influences investigative reporting, especially on large infrastructure, public tenders, and asset ownership. Future legislative actions on press freedom will also be critical indicators of Nigeria's evolving governance landscape.
