Analysis indicates a weaker U.S. dollar is anticipated in the second half of 2026. This shift is expected to significantly improve capital flows into emerging markets and bolster local currencies against the dollar.

For investors in Ghana, Nigeria, Kenya, Malawi, and Uganda, this represents a crucial signal. A weaker dollar translates to reduced foreign exchange risk, potentially enhancing returns on local currency investments when repatriated. It also makes regional assets more attractive to international capital, supporting valuations and potentially easing access to funding.

Monitor U.S. economic indicators, Federal Reserve policy statements, and real-time capital flow data into our target African markets. Early indicators of dollar depreciation or increased portfolio inflows should prompt a review of current allocations and potential entry points ahead of H2 2026.
