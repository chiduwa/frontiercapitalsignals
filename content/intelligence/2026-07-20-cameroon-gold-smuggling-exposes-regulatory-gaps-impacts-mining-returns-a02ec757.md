An investigation reveals that barely 5% of Cameroon's gold production passes through legal channels. The overwhelming majority is illicitly smuggled out, often through international airports, highlighting severe systemic vulnerabilities in the country's security and regulatory oversight.

For international investors, this widespread leakage directly translates to significant state revenue loss and undermines official economic data. It signals a high-risk environment for legitimate mining operations due to weak governance, unfair competition from illegal actors, and potential reputational hazards for those operating in the region. This situation impacts market transparency and data reliability.

Investors should closely monitor any governmental reforms aimed at strengthening border control, formalizing artisanal mining, and enhancing regulatory enforcement within the extractives sector. Watch for broader regional initiatives to improve mineral supply chain traceability and combat corruption, as these will indicate commitment to a more stable investment climate.
