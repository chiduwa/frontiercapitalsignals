Conakry has announced a policy to cease raw gold exports, pivoting towards domestic refining. This strategic shift aims to capture greater in-country value from its significant mineral wealth, moving beyond raw material extraction.

This presents substantial opportunities for investors in gold refining infrastructure, processing technology, and associated logistics. It fundamentally alters Guinea's position in the global gold supply chain, creating new demand for specific expertise and capital, provided the country can successfully build this capacity.

Investors should closely monitor policy specifics, financing availability, and tangible progress in infrastructure development. Evaluating the regulatory framework and the pace of actual capacity building will be critical indicators for market entry and understanding long-term viability.
