Ghana's Food and Agriculture Minister, Eric Opoku, is strongly advocating for increased national investment in agro-processing. This push aims to tackle persistent farm gluts, significantly reduce post-harvest losses, and generate employment opportunities for youth.

This signals clear government intent to prioritize and potentially support the agro-processing sector. Investors should note the growing policy tailwinds that could translate into favorable incentives, infrastructure development, or direct funding for companies addressing these national challenges. Local players like P&A African Food are already seeking to scale.

We should watch for specific policy frameworks, budget allocations, or public-private partnership initiatives designed to stimulate this sector. Track any investment announcements from local or international players, and monitor the government's follow-through on reducing post-harvest losses and boosting processing capacity.
