After a period of onshore divestment, international oil majors are now committing billions to Nigeria's deep offshore sector. This represents a significant pivot, signalling renewed confidence in large-scale upstream investments within the country.

This shift is driven by Nigeria's revamped regulatory framework and new fiscal incentives, making deepwater exploration more attractive. It de-risks substantial capital deployment, potentially unlocking billions of barrels and bolstering Nigeria's long-term energy output and foreign exchange earnings. This improved climate for mega-projects signals broader opportunities for significant capital.

Investors should closely track the execution timelines and early production forecasts from these deepwater projects. Monitor the impact on Nigeria's crude oil export volumes and FX liquidity. Further, observe if this renewed major-player interest in energy translates into similar policy reforms and large-scale investment uptake in other strategic sectors.
