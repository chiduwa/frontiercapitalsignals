Malawi Defence Force Commander General Jaffu disclosed that the military aircraft involved in the fatal crash, which killed former Vice President Chilima and eight others, was allegedly hired based on verbal orders without any formal written request from the Office of the President and Cabinet. This indicates a significant procedural breakdown at the highest levels.

This reliance on informal, verbal directives at high levels points to potential governance weaknesses and opacity. Such practices undermine institutional predictability and the rule of law, critical factors for investor confidence and assessing operational risk within the country.

Investors should closely track the inquiry's outcome for accountability and any subsequent reforms targeting procurement and governance protocols. The government's reaction to these revelations will be a key indicator of its commitment to transparency and strengthening formal institutional processes, directly influencing the investment climate.
