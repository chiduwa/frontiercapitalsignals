Malawi's Reserve Bank introduced a new licensing regime for foreign exchange bureaus, featuring shorter cycles, higher capital requirements, and spot-only trading. The bank frames this as a move towards a cleaner, more transparent market. However, critics suggest these reforms do not tackle the fundamental issue of chronic forex scarcity.

For investors, this signals continued difficulty in repatriating profits and accessing foreign currency for operational needs. While transparency is welcome, the reforms address symptoms, not the root cause. Expect persistent FX volatility and potential currency depreciation, highlighting an unstable environment for capital deployment and returns.

Monitor the practical impact of these reforms on actual forex availability and the official versus parallel market rates. Observe how effectively the RBM enforces compliance and whether the black market shrinks. Watch for additional fiscal or monetary policies that genuinely address Malawi's underlying supply-side forex challenges.
