Zambia's governing UPND party under President Hichilema boasts significant economic achievements, driven by a booming mining sector and record agricultural harvests. Despite these gains, a growing segment of the youth expresses deep dissatisfaction with the stalled pace of democratic reforms and perceived backsliding on freedoms.

For investors, this presents a nuanced landscape. The strong economic fundamentals, particularly in key sectors like mining and agriculture, indicate attractive growth potential and commodity-driven returns. However, the rising social and political discontent among the next generation introduces an element of heightened political risk, potentially impacting long-term stability, policy predictability, and investor confidence.

Moving forward, investors should closely monitor the government's engagement with youth grievances and any movement on democratic reform commitments. Watch for shifts in public sentiment, potential for social unrest, and how these dynamics may influence the political landscape leading up to future elections, which could signal policy continuity or disruptive change.
