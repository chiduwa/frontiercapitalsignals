An Op-ed highlights Africa's growing ambition to control its digital future, underscoring the critical need to retain more value from its data. The piece, indirectly spurred by a Microsoft tax report, emphasizes building robust local cloud and artificial intelligence infrastructure across the continent.

This imperative presents significant investment opportunities. Fund managers should target companies developing localized data centers, AI solutions, and broader digital infrastructure that supports data sovereignty. AfCFTA's framework stands to unify regulatory landscapes, fostering a more cohesive and attractive continental digital market for these ventures.

Monitor government policies on data localization, incentives for local cloud adoption, and AI development in key markets like Kenya, Nigeria, and Ghana. Also, track AfCFTA's progress in harmonizing digital trade and data policies; these will define the regulatory environment and scaling potential for pan-African digital investments.
