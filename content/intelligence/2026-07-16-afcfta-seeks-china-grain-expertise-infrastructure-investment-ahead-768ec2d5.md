The African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA) is engaging with China to acquire specialized expertise in grain storage and processing. This initiative addresses Africa's long-standing challenge of insufficient infrastructure to efficiently move food from farms to markets, despite vast agricultural potential.

This signals a continent-wide push to improve agricultural value chains. Investors should note emerging opportunities in modernizing logistics, storage facilities, and processing plants across AfCFTA member states. Chinese involvement could facilitate funding mechanisms, technology transfer, and joint venture partnerships in these critical sectors.

Monitor for specific policy announcements, investment frameworks, and pilot projects resulting from these discussions. Look for tenders and partnership opportunities in agricultural infrastructure development, particularly in Ghana, Nigeria, Kenya, Malawi, and Uganda, as these solutions scale up across the continent.
