Cameroon is losing billions from its gold sector due to extensive illegal mining, with state revenues severely impacted. Porous borders and high-level complicity facilitate this illicit trade, resulting in gold mining contributing barely 1% to the national GDP despite the country's rich resources.

This situation highlights profound governance risks and a challenging operating environment for legitimate mining investments across the broader African continent. For international investors evaluating resource opportunities, it underscores the critical importance of robust regulatory frameworks, transparent enforcement, and political stability to safeguard capital and ensure predictable returns.

Investors should monitor any concrete steps taken by the Cameroonian government to address this systemic issue. Key indicators include new policies on artisanal mining, enhanced border security, and genuine prosecution efforts against those involved in illicit trade. Regional cooperation on resource governance and anti-corruption measures will also signal potential improvements for future sector stability.
