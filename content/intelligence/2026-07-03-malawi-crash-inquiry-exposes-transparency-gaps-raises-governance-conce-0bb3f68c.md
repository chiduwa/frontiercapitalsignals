A witness testifying before Malawi's parliamentary inquiry into the Chikangawa plane crash stated he knew Vice President Chilima was dead hours before any public announcement. He reportedly received strikingly detailed information from a retired military general, suggesting significant discrepancies in official communication surrounding the incident.

For investors, this testimony directly questions government transparency and crisis communication protocols. A perceived delay or lack of candor from official channels can erode confidence in institutional reliability and governance standards. Such uncertainty can increase perceived political risk and impact the broader investment climate.

We will watch the full findings of this inquiry closely, along with the government's official response and any subsequent accountability measures. Monitor the broader political landscape for signs of instability or shifts in public confidence, as the handling of these revelations will be key to signaling commitment to good governance.
