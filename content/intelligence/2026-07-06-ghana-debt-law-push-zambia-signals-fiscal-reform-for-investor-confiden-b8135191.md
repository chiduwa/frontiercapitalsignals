Zambia's Minister of Finance, Situmbeko Musokotwane, publicly advised Ghana to enact restrictive laws on debt accumulation. He highlighted Zambia's own legal framework, which empowers parliament with greater oversight and limitations on government borrowing, effectively tying the executive's hands for improved fiscal discipline.

This advisory carries significant weight, coming from a nation that recently underwent extensive debt restructuring. It signals a growing regional commitment to fiscal responsibility across our target markets. For Ghana, implementing similar robust legislation could enhance macroeconomic stability, improve investor confidence by reducing sovereign risk, and potentially lead to better credit ratings.
