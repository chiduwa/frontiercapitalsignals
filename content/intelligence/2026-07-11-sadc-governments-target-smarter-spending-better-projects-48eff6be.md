SADC nations recently gathered over 140 officials for a regional public investment seminar. The focus was improving public spending decisions and maximizing limited resources through evidence-based policymaking, a growing trend across Southern Africa.

This initiative is critical for investors. As governments face rising debt and climate demands, a commitment to efficient, data-driven spending signals greater fiscal discipline and potentially more stable operating environments. It suggests a move towards more predictable project selection and execution, reducing waste and enhancing returns on capital deployed in the region.

Investors should watch for tangible outcomes. This includes specific policy frameworks emerging, clearer project pipelines, and how evidence-based criteria influence budget allocations at the national level, particularly within SADC members like Malawi. Improved governance here could unlock significant long-term value.
