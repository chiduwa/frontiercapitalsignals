Crude oil prices dipped below $90 a barrel on Tuesday. This followed reports indicating potential ceasefire talks between the United States and Iran, suggesting a possible de-escalation of Middle East tensions and a potential increase in global supply.

For investors in Nigeria, this development is critical. As Africa's largest oil producer, lower crude prices directly impact government revenues, foreign exchange earnings, and the stability of the Naira. While importing nations like Kenya and Ghana might benefit from reduced fuel costs, Nigeria faces significant fiscal pressure.

Monitor the progress of US-Iran diplomatic efforts and their impact on global oil supply. Investors should also watch the Nigerian government's fiscal policy responses and the Central Bank's measures to manage foreign exchange stability amidst potential revenue shortfalls.
