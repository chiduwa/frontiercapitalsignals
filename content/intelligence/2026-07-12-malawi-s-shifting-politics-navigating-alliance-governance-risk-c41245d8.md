Malawi's political landscape is fundamentally changing. The era of single-party dominance has ended, replaced by an increasingly fragmented electorate driven by diverse geographic, demographic, economic, and identity-based interests. This necessitates broader and more complex political alliances to form governments.

For investors, this shift indicates a higher probability of coalition governments, which can introduce policy instability and longer implementation timelines. We anticipate potential shifts in regulatory frameworks and economic priorities as these alliances form and dissolve, elevating political risk. Policy consistency is paramount for long-term capital deployment.

Our immediate focus is on the stability and composition of new political alliances, particularly their influence on critical policy areas affecting foreign investment. Monitor upcoming election cycles for signs of durable coalitions or continued fragmentation, and how this translates into effective governance and economic predictability.
